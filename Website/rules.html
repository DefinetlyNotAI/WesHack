Comprehensive Hackathon Rules Document

1. Guidelines

a) Participation

i) Team Size

  • Maximum of 4 members per team.

ii) Team Behavior

  • All participants must act respectfully.
  • Professional conduct is mandatory during the event.
  • Violations result in disqualification or removal.

iii) Inclusivity

  • Open to all backgrounds and skill levels.
  • Exclusionary behavior results in immediate disqualification.

iv) Attendance

  • All team members must attend both competition days.
  • Absence may result in disqualification from prizes.

v) Reporting Violations

  • Violations can be reported anonymously.
  • Organizers will investigate all reports.
  • Contact organizers for issues related to misconduct.

b) Team Formation

i) Pre-formed Teams

  • No random teams formed by organizers.
  • Teams must be finalized before the competition.

ii) Confirmation

  • All team members must confirm their participation before the start date.
  • Ensure all teammates understand rules and expectations.

c) Project Development

i) Original Work

  • All code must be written during the hackathon.
  • Pre-existing libraries can be used unless prohibited.
  • External dependencies must be documented.

ii) Reuse of Past Projects

  • Reusing up to 60% of prior work is permitted.
  • Clear documentation must explain the percentage reused.

iii) AI Usage

  • AI may assist but cannot dominate the project.
  • Teams must clearly state how AI was used in the README.
  • Excessive AI reliance leads to disqualification.

iv) Prohibited Work

  • No pre-event coding except reusable portions under the 60% rule.
  • Pre-written code must be labeled and explained in the README.

2. Submission Guidelines

a) Deadline

  • Projects must be submitted by 14th Dec 2024.

b) Required Deliverables

  • Source code with documentation.
  • README file (including description, technologies, AI use, etc.).
  • Presentation slides (if applicable).

c) Documentation

  • Clear documentation will increase your chances of winning.
  • Ensure ease of understanding and replication.

3. Judging Criteria

a) Innovation

  • Novelty and originality of the idea.
  • Differentiation from existing solutions.

b) Functionality

  • Does the project meet its objectives?
  • Are there bugs or limitations?

c) Design

  • User interface design and usability.
  • Accessibility for users of all skill levels.

d) Impact

  • Addressing real-world problems.
  • Potential reach and influence.

e) Technical Merit

  • Quality of the project’s architecture.
  • Technical innovation or achievements.

f) Documentation

  • Thoroughness of the documentation.
  • Ease of understanding for external users.

g) Additional Criteria

  • Judges may apply extra criteria at their discretion.

4. Tools & Technology

a) Approved Tools

  • Any language, framework, or tool is allowed unless prohibited.
  • Organizers may restrict certain tools for security reasons.

b) Hardware Usage

  • Must declare hardware before the event.
  • Teams must follow safety protocols when using hardware.

c) Internet Usage

  • Internet allowed for research, not external assistance.
  • No communication with people outside the competition.

5. Intellectual Property

a) Ownership

  • Participants retain ownership of their projects.
  • Judges can review and analyze the source code.

b) Licensing

  • All projects must be fully open-source.
  • The README must include a license or state LICENSE N/A.

c) Patent Rights

  • The Hackathon's Organisers reserves the right to patent any project created during the hackathon.

6. Prizes & Awards

a) Prize Eligibility

  • All team members must follow the rules.
  • Teams must be present on both competition days.
  • Availability for post-event interviews if required.

b) Award Announcement

  • Winners announced on the final competition day.
  • Runner-ups may receive recognition.

c) Cash Prizes

  • Information about cash prizes is available here.

d) Post-Event Obligations

  • Winners may be asked to participate in promotional activities.

7. Conclusion

  • By participating, you agree to follow the hackathon's rules and guidelines. Failure to comply may lead to disqualification. Contact the organizers with any questions or concerns.

Good luck and happy programming!
